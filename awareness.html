Spreading Awareness

Teaching people about human trafficking is key to preventing it. Social media, school programs, and public campaigns help people learn the warning signs and encourage them to report suspicious activities. Groups like the Ontario Coalition Against Human Trafficking and the Canadian Centre to End Human Trafficking focus on educating people and supporting victims. Local communities can make a big difference by getting involved.

How to Get Help

There are many resources in Ontario to support victims and stop trafficking:

  • Hotlines: Call the Canadian Human Trafficking Hotline at 1-833-900-1010 for help anytime.
  • Safe Places: Shelters like Covenant House in Toronto provide victims with safety and support.
  • Counseling: Survivors can get therapy and emotional support through groups like Victim Services Ontario.
  • Education: Schools and community programs teach young people how to stay safe. Click this link to be taken to a list of services in Ontario.
